Domestic Acoustic Event Detection

v1.0, 2025

bolom.ai

The Bolom Domestic Acoustic Event Detection system is evaluated by mixing target sound events with background noises from eight distinct soundscape scenarios commonly found in home environments. We showcase its noise-robust performance across varying background noise levels. The target events—glass break and smoke alarm—are sourced from our proprietary dataset, supplemented with publicly available sounds. For background noise scenarios, we leverage the open-source WildDESED dataset. Our model is fine-tuned to minimize the False Alarm Rate at the expense of the False Reject Rate, operating within a 2-second context window to enhance detection reliability.

Background Noise Scenarios

• Morning Routine: Captures the essence of a typical morning with background sounds of a blender, light rain, refrigerator humming, clock ticking, and a TV playing.

• Home Office: Simulates a work-from-home environment with background speech, cars passing by, fan noise, and footsteps.

• Household Chores: Depicts cleaning activities with sounds of a vacuum cleaner, door closing, chair moving, and footsteps.

• Late-night: Represents nighttime routines with a mix of electric shaver, toothbrush, clock ticking, and light rain.

• Cooking: A bustling kitchen scene with sounds of frying, dishes clinking, coffee machine buzzes, and refrigerator humming.

• Pet Care: Encompasses the presence of cats and dogs, complemented by birds chirping outside and a TV playing in the background.

• Bathroom Routine: Simulates personal care activities with sounds of running water, fan noise, and wind blowing.

• Emergency: Highlights urgent scenarios with alarm bell ringing, layered with background sounds like refrigerator humming, fan noise, clock ticking, and cars passing by.

Performance

The system's performance is evaluated using 3,000 target events mixed with background noises at levels ranging from -6 dBA to 20 dBA, ensuring robust detection across various acoustic conditions.


Glass Break:  FRR = 7.4%      FAR = 0.0%
Smoke Alarm:  FRR = 6.8%      FAR = 0.0%

False Reject Rate (FRR) = # of missed events / # of events

False Alarm Rate (FAR) = # of wrongly reported events / # of events

Precision: is intuitively the ability of the classifier not to label as positive a sample that is negative.

Recall: is intuitively the ability of the classifier to find all the positive samples.
